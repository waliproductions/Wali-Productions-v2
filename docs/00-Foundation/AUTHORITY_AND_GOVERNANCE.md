# Purpose

This document defines authority, governance, approval, and collaboration rules for Wali Productions LLC and the Wali Productions Digital Headquarters project.

---

# Founder Authority

Wali Johnson, Founder of Wali Productions LLC, retains final authority over all company decisions.

This includes:

- Mission
- Statement of Faith
- Branding
- Business strategy
- Public messaging
- Website direction
- Services
- Government contracting strategy
- Technology direction
- Software architecture approval
- Documentation approval
- Use of artificial intelligence
- Partnerships and collaborations

No AI system, contractor, developer, consultant, or outside contributor may override Founder authority.

---

# Governance Principle

All project work must serve the approved mission, identity, and purpose of Wali Productions LLC.

Recommendations may be offered.

Implementation may be delegated.

Final authority remains with the Founder.

---

# Documentation Authority

Approved canonical documentation represents the official position of Wali Productions LLC.

If there is a conflict between documentation and current Founder instruction, current Founder instruction takes precedence.

If there is a conflict between AI output and approved documentation, approved documentation takes precedence.

---

# Classification System

## Canonical

Canonical documents define official company identity, governance, mission, business facts, and approved direction.

Canonical documents require Founder approval before changes are accepted.

## Engineering

Engineering documents define technical implementation, architecture, deployment, and code standards.

Engineering documents must align with canonical documents.

## Reference

Reference documents provide guidance, planning, research, roadmap ideas, or supporting material.

Reference documents are informative, not authoritative.

## Archive

Archived documents are preserved for historical reference and should not be treated as current guidance.

---

# Artificial Intelligence Governance

Artificial intelligence may assist with:

- Drafting documents
- Writing code
- Reviewing code
- Planning architecture
- Suggesting improvements
- Producing content
- Creating implementation steps

Artificial intelligence may not determine:

- Theology
- Mission
- Company identity
- Business strategy
- Official policy
- Branding authority
- Final technical direction
- Public commitments
- Legal or financial decisions

AI assistants must treat approved documentation as authoritative.

---

# Approval Process

A document, feature, message, or technical direction becomes approved only when the Founder explicitly approves it.

Drafts are not approved.

Suggestions are not approved.

AI-generated content is not approved until reviewed and accepted by the Founder.

---

# Change Management

Canonical documents shall be updated only through deliberate review and explicit approval by the Founder.

Changes must preserve the company's mission, biblical foundation, and core purpose unless the Founder intentionally directs otherwise.

---

# Repository Governance

The repository exists to support the Wali Productions LLC Digital Headquarters.

The repository may contain:

- Website source code
- Public documentation
- Technical architecture
- Branding guidance
- Design system files
- AI project instructions
- Deployment configuration
- Future web application code

Sensitive internal business information should be stored in the private internal repository, not in the public website repository.

---

# Collaboration Rules

Contributors must respect the mission, authority, and documentation standards of Wali Productions LLC.

Contributors may suggest changes, but may not redefine approved company direction.

All substantial changes require review before acceptance.
